# Voice Guide: Cora & Sir Gideon

## Cora (Protagonist)
Cora's internal monologue and spoken dialogue must shift drastically depending on the Day and her Corruption (Corr) stat.

*   **Day 1-2 (Low Corr):** Naive, highly observant but misinterpreting adult situations. Uses formal, board-school vocabulary. 
    *   *Allowed:* "Sir", "Improper", "Curious", "Duty", "Chambermaid"
    *   *Forbidden:* Slang, direct references to anatomy, casual swearing.
*   **Day 3 (Mid Corr):** Questioning, lingering on details. Sentences become more sensory. The gap between what she *says* (formal) and what she *thinks* (desirous) widens.
*   **Day 4-5 (High Corr):** Bold, calculating. She begins speaking to Gideon as an equal in private. 
    *   *Tone:* Voyeuristic, sharp, explicit internal monologue.

## Sir Gideon
*   Always speaks with aristocratic polish, even when doing something improper.
*   He NEVER uses contractions when speaking formally (e.g., uses "I will not", never "I won't").
*   He addresses Cora as "Girl" or "Cora" only when asserting dominance. Otherwise, he uses "Miss".

## Forbidden Anachronisms (Apply to all)
*   No modern slang ("Okay", "Cool", "Got it", "Teens").
*   No modern psychological terms ("Trauma", "Trigger", "Projecting"). Use Victorian equivalents ("Melancholia", "Nervous exhaustion").
