# Forensic Psychology Profile Report - Missy

## What changed
- Promoted Missy from minor notes to a fully independent, locked canon profile file: `narrative/canon/missy_character_canon.md`.
- Established her structural role as the "Mirror Self" / horror movie trope duality (Missy is the simple country girl Cora is pretending to be, looking back with a mischievous grin in the glass).
- Detailed her psychological tension: naive country curiosity processed through religious sin/shame language, eager for approval but drawn to the fire of transgressive desire.
- Defined Cora's branching paths with Missy: either protecting her as a proxy for Cora's former innocent self (incurring precarity) or steering her to discard religious fear and embrace her smart, curious self (creating a highly charged, dangerous peer dynamic).
- Standardized her voice guide: naive register, religious vocabulary, breathy suggestive shift.

## Why it changed
- Grounding Missy's innocence as a direct mirror and ticking clock for Cora's own past self elevates the narrative stakes of Cora's choices.
- The Level 3 spice integration connects her unawakened curiosity directly to the suggestive atmosphere of the Savoy.

## Future writing implications
- Missy is never a caricature of simple-mindedness. She has an active, latent intelligence trying to decode the world.
- Every time Cora uses her or manipulates her, there must be a tangible accumulation of emotional cost (the "Missy Debt").
- If steered toward awakening, her voice and posture must gradually shift to mirror Cora's mockingly knowing intelligence.
