## CHARACTER DEFINITION: CORA HARTLEY (CANON)

Project: Untitled Victorian VN  
Role: Protagonist / Player Character  
Archetype: The Undercover Observer / The Emerging Sovereign

---

### 1. Core History and Background
- Current Station: Domestic Servant (Maid) at the Savoy.
- True Nature: An incognito writer gathering material through forged references.
- Catalyst: Circumstances (necessity rather than ambition) forced her into this covert position. She uses her immediate environment as source material for a manuscript that functions as both record and manifesto.
- Social Status: Externally, she is at the bottom of the Victorian social hierarchy; internally, she operates with detached, analytical intelligence.

### 2. Attributes and Psychological Profile
- Hyper-observant and taxonomic: she catalogs people and power structures as material.
- Aphoristic intelligence: she thinks compositionally and edits events into narrative logic.
- Unsentimental accounting: she tracks cost, guilt, and consequence without self-pity.
- Composed under pressure: fear compresses spoken output rather than unraveling it.
- Dry private humor: irony stays in narration, never performed outwardly.

### 3. Dual Motivations (Survival Modes)
Mode A: Final Girl (Driven by Necessity)
- Motivation: survival and protection of what matters.
- Worldview: conscience has a cost, but she pays it to remain alive and functional.
- Internal register: guilt-burdened but resolute.

Mode B: Walter White (Driven by Capability)
- Motivation: intellectual dominance and exposure of social fraud.
- Worldview: danger shifts from being caught to enjoying the exercise of hidden competence.
- Internal register: ruthless self-actualization.

### 4. Voice and Dialogue Mechanic
- Defining mechanic is the gap between internal monologue (complex, analytical, sensory) and spoken dialogue (short, deferential, performed).
- The gap narrows in private high-stakes contexts across the five-day arc but remains strategically useful in public.

### 5. Key Relationships and Dynamics
- Stern and Vance: structural obstacles and study subjects within hierarchy.
- Peers and staff: managed with tactical warmth and low-exposure phrasing.
- Gideon: primary foil and recognition axis; progression runs from defensive formality (Day 1) to strategic testing (Day 4) to private persona drop (Day 5).
