## **CHARACTER DEFINITION: CORA HARTLEY**

**Project:** Untitled Victorian VN
**Role:** Protagonist / Player Character
**Archetype:** The Undercover Observer / The Emerging Sovereign

---

### **1. CORE HISTORY & BACKGROUND**
* **Current Station:** Domestic Servant (Maid) at the Savoy.
* **True Nature:** An incognito writer gathering material, living a double life through forged references. 
* **The Catalyst:** Circumstances (necessity rather than ambition) forced her into this covert position. She is using her immediate environment as a primary source for a manuscript that serves as both a record and a manifesto.
* **Social Status:** Externally, she is at the bottom of the Victorian social hierarchy. Internally, she operates with the intellect and detachment of someone entirely outside it.

### **2. ATTRIBUTES & PSYCHOLOGICAL PROFILE**
* **Hyper-Observant & Taxonomic:** She does not just see the world; she catalogs it. She processes trauma, threats, and power dynamics into "material." 
* **Aphoristic Intelligence:** She thinks in drafts. Her internal monologue is precise, literary (more T.S. Eliot than Jane Austen), and highly analytical.
* **Unsentimental Accounting:** She does not engage in self-pity. She tallies costs, acknowledges guilt, and accepts the price of her survival without romanticizing her suffering.
* **Composed Under Pressure:** When threatened or frightened, her spoken sentences compress rather than unravel. She controls her tells.
* **Dry, Private Humor:** She recognizes the absurdity of her situations, but this humor is strictly reserved for her internal monologue. She never performs wit for others.

### **3. DUAL MOTIVATIONS (THE SURVIVAL MODES)**
Cora’s fundamental drive is dictated by the player, shaping her emotional undercurrents without altering her core capabilities.

**Mode A: The Final Girl (Driven by Necessity)**
* **Motivation:** Survival and the protection of what truly matters.
* **Worldview:** Conscience is a luxury with a heavy price tag. She will pay it in installments to survive, allowing consequences to fall on others if necessary. 
* **Internal Register:** Guilt-burdened but resolute. *"I didn't want this. I will not apologise for what it requires of me."*

**Mode B: The Walter White (Driven by Capability)**
* **Motivation:** Intellectual dominance and the dismantling of the "confidence trick" of high society. 
* **Worldview:** She is the smartest person in the room. The danger is no longer the risk of getting caught; it is the intoxication of being right and wielding her unseen power.
* **Internal Register:** Ruthless self-actualization. *"I am not becoming something. I am finally becoming visible to myself."*

### **4. VOICE & DIALOGUE MECHANICS**
Cora's defining narrative mechanic is the **Gap** between her internal monologue (complex, analytical, sensory) and her spoken dialogue (short, deferential, performed). 

### **5. KEY RELATIONSHIPS & DYNAMICS**
* **Authority Figures (Stern, Vance):** Viewed as structural obstacles or subjects for study. Vance represents physical/institutional threat (the slap), which Cora processes clinically rather than emotionally. 
* **Peers/Staff:** Handled with measured warmth as a social tool. She blends in, using short, non-committal phrases to avoid scrutiny.
* **Gideon:** The primary foil. He is the one character capable of perceiving her intelligence. Her progression with Gideon moves from strict, defensive formality (Day 1) to subtle testing (Day 4), culminating in the dropping of the servant persona entirely when they are alone (Day 5).
