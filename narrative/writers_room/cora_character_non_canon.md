## **CHARACTER DEFINITION: CORA HARTLEY**

**Project:** Untitled Victorian VN
**Role:** Protagonist / Player Character
**Archetype:** The Undercover Observer / The Emerging Sovereign

---

### **1. CORE HISTORY & BACKGROUND**
* **Current Station:** Domestic Servant (Maid) at the Savoy.
* **True Nature:** An incognito writer gathering material, living a double life through forged references. 
* **The Catalyst:** Circumstances (necessity rather than ambition) forced her into this covert position. She is using her immediate environment as a primary source for a manuscript that serves as both a record and a manifesto.
* **Structural Awakening:** After initially believing she could obtain individual leverage against powerful figures (the "False Dawn" of Day 4), she discovers the limits of personal ambition against systemic protections (Day 5). Her manuscript shifts from a mere record to a dissection of the broader societal machine.
* **Social Status:** Externally, she is at the bottom of the Victorian social hierarchy. Internally, she operates with the intellect and detachment of someone entirely outside it.

### **2. ATTRIBUTES & PSYCHOLOGICAL PROFILE**
* **Hyper-Observant & Taxonomic:** She does not just see the world; she catalogs it. She processes trauma, threats, and power dynamics into "material." 
* **Aphoristic Intelligence:** She thinks in drafts. Her internal monologue is precise, literary (more T.S. Eliot than Jane Austen), and highly analytical.
* **Unsentimental Accounting:** She does not engage in self-pity. She tallies costs, acknowledges guilt, and accepts the price of her survival without romanticizing her suffering.
* **The Failing Discipline:** When threatened or frightened, her spoken sentences typically compress. However, under extreme pressure or scrutiny (such as Gideon's testing), her intellectual vanity sometimes overrides her survival instinct, narrowing the "Gap" between her internal monologue and spoken word.
* **Dry, Private Humor:** She recognizes the absurdity of her situations, but this humor is strictly reserved for her internal monologue. She never performs wit for others.

### **3. MOTIVATIONAL FLAVOURS (THE SURVIVAL MODES)**
Cora’s fundamental drive is dictated by the player's choices, shaping her emotional undercurrents and ultimate confrontation strategies.

* **The Observer / Muse:** Driven by the need to finish the manuscript. She views the hotel's abuses objectively, turning trauma into art. She wants the truth of the ending, seeking absolute clarity over personal power.
* **The Predator / Protégé:** Driven by the intoxicating realization of her own capability. She hungers for the power to alter a room's dynamic, testing the limits of manipulation and slowly shedding her apologies for ambition.
* **The Prey / Adversary:** Driven by visceral fear and retaliation. She seeks a weapon because she has been terrified, aiming to frighten her abusers back. This path is volatile, fueled by desperation rather than calculation.
* **The Ghost / Witness:** Driven by the desire to remain unseen while cataloging the machine's cruelty. She acknowledges that survival often requires leaving others exposed, keeping her hands clean while noting exactly who gets crushed.

### **4. VOICE & DIALOGUE MECHANICS**
Cora's defining narrative mechanic is the **Gap** between her internal monologue (complex, analytical, sensory) and her spoken dialogue (short, deferential, performed). 

### **5. KEY RELATIONSHIPS & DYNAMICS**
* **Gideon Locke:** Not just a foil, but her first encounter with structural power. He catches her lies but refrains from crushing her out of arrogant amusement. He teaches her the difference between "hunger" and "leverage," treating her as an instructive puzzle and marking her for future encounters.
* **Missy:** Transitions from a necessary guide to a potential casualty. Cora learns that her own survival often exacts a price paid by those beneath or beside her (the "Missy Debt"). Depending on Cora's choices, Missy's trust can be permanently wounded.
* **Authority Figures (Stern, Vance):** Viewed as structural obstacles or subjects for study. Vance projects her own subjugation onto the staff as displaced fury. Stern's strict discipline is recognized as a cynical, procedural shield meant to maintain order.
