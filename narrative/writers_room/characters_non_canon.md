## Ms. Stern
- **Role:** Head of Housekeeping; operational authority over staff discipline.
- **Public register:** concise, procedural, reputation-protective.
- **Private register:** controlled concern; rarely overtly emotional.
- **Core dynamic with Cora:** suspicious of deviation, respects competence, protects hotel order first. By Day 3/4, she warns Cora that "a guest's attention is not a promotion," revealing her strict discipline as a cynical but pragmatic shield for the staff against the upper class.

## Missy
- **Role:** junior maid / innocence mirror for Cora.
- **Public register:** eager, chatty, deferential, occasionally overfamiliar.
- **Private register:** anxious moral framing (sin/guilt language), seeks reassurance.
- **Core dynamic with Cora:** friend-pawn duality; trust vector for manipulation or protection branches. She becomes a casualty of Cora's ambition ("Missy Debt"), carrying the emotional consequences when Cora uses her as cover or forces her to lie. Her trust in Cora can be permanently wounded.

## Gideon Locke
- **Role:** Wealthy guest, structural antagonist, and catalyst for Cora's awakening.
- **Public register:** Precise, commanding, uses space and silence as weapons. Never needs to yell.
- **Private register:** Arrogant, curious, amused by attempts at leverage. He treats people as puzzles or instruments.
- **Core dynamic with Cora:** He catches her intelligence and her crimes but doesn't destroy her out of sheer overconfidence. He marks her for future interaction, serving as a mirror that teaches Cora the limits of individual hunger versus systemic, structural power.

## Vance
- **Role:** Gideon's companion.
- **Public register:** Entitled, sharp, performative authority.
- **Private register:** Fragile anger, deeply humiliated by her submission to Gideon.
- **Core dynamic with Cora:** She views Cora and the staff as targets for her displaced fury. She represents the physical and emotional violence of the class structure, projecting her own powerlessness onto those below her.
