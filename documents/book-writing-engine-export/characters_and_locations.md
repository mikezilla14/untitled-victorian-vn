# CHARACTERS & LOCATIONS TRANSPOSITION DATABASE

This document contains the canonical profiles of the cast and settings, mapping their real-life (IRL) identities at the Savoy Hotel to their fictional transpositions in Cora Hartley's penny dreadful manuscript (`book1`).

---

## 1. Character Transposition Index

### protagonist: CORA HARTLEY $\rightarrow$ CORALIE VALE
* **Class & Role (IRL):** Irish chambermaid at the Savoy Hotel, London. Hyper-literate undercover writer.
* **Role in Book:** *Coralie Vale*, a Wiltshire country girl who becomes a domestic servant at the gothic estates of Alderwood and Ravenshade.
* **Backstory & Origins:** Born into rural Irish poverty. She presenting as "Black Irish" (dark hair, deep piercing dark eyes). Victorian prejudice forces her to mask her origin by performing as an English country girl (Flawless Sussex/Wiltshire drawl). Dismissed from Sir John Wiltshire's estate after trespassing in Lady Eleanor's quarters.
* **Psychological Profile:** Hyper-observant, taxonomic, and unsentimentally clinical. She catalogs people, appetites, and power structures as material. Composed under pressure, turning terror into velocity on the page. She has a strong survival instinct; she is willing to sacrifice peers (the "Missy Debt") to protect her identity.
* **Voice & Dialogue Constraints:**
  - **Monologue:** Complex, literary, sensory, and highly analytical.
  - **Dialogue:** Short, deferential, and formal. She monitors every syllable, suppressing her Irish accent unless highly provoked or challenged intellectually.

---

### deuteragonist: SIR GIDEON LOCKE $\rightarrow$ LORD CALDOR
* **Class & Role (IRL):** Wealthy, aristocratic guest occupying a premium suite at the Savoy Hotel. Member of transgressive elite salons.
* **Role in Book:** *Lord Caldor*, the cold, pale aristocrat of Ravenshade and Alderwood, acting as Coralie's dark patron and sovereign predator.
* **Psychological Profile:** Possesses a clinical, detached, and brilliant intellect. He views human relations as chess and transaction. He does not view desire as a moral failing but as a lever. He is motivated by the psychological submission of others, seeking the moment of absolute capitulation when a subject discards their mask. His intellectual vanity is his primary hazard; he welcomes attempts at leverage.
* **Voice & Dialogue Constraints:**
  - **High-Compression Register:** Short, declarative sentences. Minimal adjectives. Speaks with the quiet weight of institutional authority.
  - **No Rhetorical Questions:** He never asks questions for information; his questions are structural traps.
  - **Suggestive Undercurrent:** Dialogue carries a highly charged subtext of command, class pressure, and physical implication.

---

### supporting cast: MS. STERN $\rightarrow$ MR. STERICK
* **Class & Role (IRL):** Head of Housekeeping at the Savoy Hotel.
* **Role in Book:** *Mr. Sterick*, the overseer and administrative warden of Alderwood/Ravenshade.
* **Backstory:** Lower-middle-class stock. Closed the door to romance in her youth after her dominant inclinations were branded unnatural. Turned her passion into a cold, structured science of control within domestic service.
* **Psychological Profile:** The sublimated dominant. Her authority is expressed through the clinical precision of her administration (inspections, schedule adjustments). She holds deep, severe maternal loyalty to her maids; she will humiliate them in private to toughen them up for predatory guests in public. She has no illusions about class morality.
* **Voice & Dialogue Constraints:**
  - **Procedural Precision:** Clipped, formal, and devoid of emotional filler. Speaks in the passive voice and uses institutional terminology to deliver personal judgments.
  - **Double Meaning:** Her talk of "impropriety" and "keeping buttons fastened" is a forensic warning about the danger of the guests' appetites.

---

### innocence mirror: MISSY $\rightarrow$ MIRI
* **Class & Role (IRL):** Junior chambermaid at the Savoy Hotel. Cora's working-class peer.
* **Role in Book:** *Miri*, a junior housemaid at Ravenshade/Alderwood who carries the curse and is sacrificed to cover the heroine's tracks.
* **Psychological Profile:** Missy represents Cora's past self, but she possesses active, self-protective boundaries. She is highly observant and intelligent but sheltered. Her religious register (fear of sin, virtue) is an active defense mechanism. She has a latent capacity for passion, but intimacy and submission are sovereign choices that must be earned through genuine romantic trust, tenderness, and respect.
* **Voice & Dialogue Constraints:**
  - **Defensive Propriety:** Polite, respectful, and anchored in religious vocabulary ("righteous," "sinful").
  - **Focused Curiosity:** In quiet moments, her voice becomes low and deliberate, showing she is actively decoding forbidden subjects.
  - **Romantic Awakening:** Warm, direct, and intimate, losing her deferential tone.

---

### class mirror: VANCE $\rightarrow$ LADY VAYNE / LADY BEATRICE
* **Class & Role (IRL):** Gideon Locke's resident companion at the Savoy.
* **Role in Book:** *Lady Vayne* (the petulant companion of Lord Caldor) and *Lady Beatrice* (the corrupted mistress of Alderwood).
* **Backstory:** Daughter of a curate or clerk. Experienced financial ruin and bartered away her autonomy for Locke's wealth.
* **Psychological Profile:** Profoundly humiliated, living in constant, fragile terror under Gideon's dominance. She sublimates her self-hatred into cruel fury and displaced dominance directed at the chambermaids. She envies Cora's internal sovereignty.
* **Voice & Dialogue Constraints:**
  - **Public Entitled Register:** Sharp, rapid-fire, high-status language. Frequent commands and dismissive terms ("girl").
  - **Private Stripped Register:** Short, breathy, hesitant dialogue when addressing Gideon, marked by apologies.
  - **Level 3 Suggestive Cruelty:** close-proximity commands (demanding Cora kneel, adjust a hem, clean spills).

---

### minor characters:
* **Sir John Wiltshire:** Late-Victorian country gentleman. Day 100 prologue authority figure who dismisses Cora at his wife's behest. Speaks with cold, quiet, procedural authority.
* **Lady Eleanor Wiltshire:** Mistress of the Wiltshire estate, caught in a secret affair with housemaid Margaret Pryce. Venomous when cornered, deploying anti-Irish slurs under panic.
* **Margaret Pryce:** Junior domestic servant at the Wiltshire estate, Lady Eleanor's partner in the scandal. Speaks in a plain service register.
